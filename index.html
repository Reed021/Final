My sophomore year was a sign that I needed to change things; my grades dipped as I realized that I loved the software aspects of Comp Sci, yet my hardware classes were a different story. Many of the elements of hardware were not only difficult for me to wrap my head around, but the way and the speed at which they were taught made it even more challenging. At the end of my Sophomore year, I consulted my advisors about my dilemma, having exhausted my efforts of constantly playing catch-up in my hardware classes.

At the end of my Sophomore year, I consulted my advisors about my dilemma, and with the help of my advisors, I determined that while my computer science skills are useful, they could be used towards a bigger purpose.
